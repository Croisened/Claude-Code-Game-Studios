01
The City That Hunts
Visual
Low-angle wide shot at street level. The run corridor stretches deep into Z-axis haze. Corporate towers flank both sides — surveillance cameras on every ledge, enforcement drones hovering dormant in the fog. Cool blue and acid yellow neon. Rain streaks diagonal. Wet chrome lane surface. No robot yet. The city breathes.
Neotropolis is alive, hostile, and watching. The player understands the stakes before they see the protagonist.

02
This One Is Yours
Visual
Medium shot, three-quarter from behind. The robot steps into frame — idle stance, upright, briefly still. Humanoid mech: geometric torso, angular limbs, wide-aperture optical sensor. Its texture set is vivid and specific — one robot out of ninety. Violet and orange neon halos its silhouette. Token ID visible as a faint HUD watermark: RR #042.
The robot is specific. It is this one — and the player recognizes it as theirs. Identity First lands.

03
The First Stride
Visual
Dynamic low-angle chase shot at knee height. Robot mid-stride: lean forward, arms pumping, optic fixed ahead. Environment rushes past in motion blur — chrome walls, neon signage streaking horizontal. Speed lines from flanking geometry. HUD live: distance counter ticking up. The world fights to keep up.
The run has begun. Speed is immediate, physical, joyful — the core fantasy delivered in motion.

04
The City Responds
Visual
Wide over-shoulder, all three lanes visible. Enforcement drone swoops from right, scanner beam cutting hot-white across center lane. Neon barrier spans left lane at waist height. Holographic checkpoint banners trigger. Red strobes from above. Collapsing architecture debris crosses right lane. Everything is aimed at the robot.
The city is actively hunting. Every obstacle reads as a deliberate enforcement action — not random scenery.
. . .
05
The City Catches Up
Visual
Tight over-shoulder, very close. Three obstacles converge simultaneously — no lane to break to. Impact. The robot's momentum carries one extra stride before collapse: controlled, fast, kinetic. It comes to rest in the center lane. Neon reflects in the wet chrome around it. The city continues to scroll for two frames after the robot stops — indifferent. Silence.
Death is cinematic and fast, not punishing. The robot went as far as it could. The city wins — this time.

06
Run Again
Visual
Score screen. The robot's ghosted silhouette occupies the left half — backlit neon, low opacity, held in the death pose. Right half: DISTANCE 0847m large, BEST 1203m in cooler color, token ID RR #042. A single highlighted button: RUN AGAIN. The neon of Neotropolis bleeds in from the edges. The gap is 356 meters. That number is all the player can think about.
Death was not the end — it was a data point. The robot is right there. The city is right there.
